Why Study in China?

China is fast becoming a top destination for international students. With hundreds of thousands of students from around the world choosing China annually, the country offers high-quality education, globally recognised degrees, and unique cultural exposure. Studying in China prepares you for a future that blends modern innovation with rich historical heritage.

Generous Scholarships & Funding

Many Chinese universities and the Chinese government provide scholarships for international students.

Universities on the Rise

Chinese universities are increasingly appearing in global rankings and gaining international reputation.

Learn Mandarin & Global Networking

Studying in China gives you the chance to learn Mandarin—one of the world’s most spoken languages—and build cross-cultural connections.

Innovative & Work-Relevant Education

China invests heavily in STEM, research, and global collaboration — offering courses aligned with modern industrial demand.

Rich Cultural Experience

Living in China gives you access to ancient heritage, bustling cities, and diverse regions — a unique backdrop for your global education.

Affordability & Value

Tuition and living costs in many Chinese cities can be more affordable than many Western destinations.

Types of Institutions

Public Universities

Government-funded universities offering a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs in English or Chinese.

Private & Specialised Institutions

Privately funded or specialised schools focusing on business, arts, design or language studies.

Vocational Colleges & Diploma Programs

Institutions offering diploma or certificate programs, often shorter in duration and more skills-focused.

Additional Benefits of Studying in China

Strong Student Support Services

Universities often provide dedicated international student offices, orientation programs, language support and accommodation assistance.

Internships & Career Experience

Many international students can access internships and part-time work opportunities in China, enhancing employability.

Vibrant Campus & City Life

Student life in Chinese cities includes rich cultural activities, travel possibilities, and exposure to new ideas.

Graduate Programs

Visa Requirement

To study in China, international students must obtain the appropriate student visa/permit. Here’s what you need to know.

  • International students planning to study in Canada must obtain a visa. The most common type is the Study Permit, designed for students enrolling in accredited colleges or universities in Canada.

Before you apply, you need:

  • Be accepted and approved for your chosen course of study.
  • Register with the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System (SEVIS) and pay a registration fee of $350 (£253).
  • You'll also need to schedule a visa interview at the Canadian embassy or consulate in your home country.

Required documents for visa interview:

  • Your passport
  • Your visa application form
  • Financial documents demonstrating your ability to fund your education and living expenses.

Visa & Permit Requirements

  • International students planning to study in China must obtain the relevant student visa (typically the X-category visa) through a Chinese consulate or embassy.

Before you apply, you should:

  • Be accepted by a Chinese university and receive the official admission letter.
  • Meet the university’s language/academic requirements and pay any required confirmation fee.
  • Submit visa application through the Chinese consulate including passport, admission letter, health certificate and proof of funds.

Required documents for visa application:

  • Valid passport
  • Visa application form and photo
  • Official admission letter from Chinese university
  • Proof of financial resources to cover your study and living costs

Merit-Based Scholarships

Awarded for academic excellence, leadership or special talents at Chinese universities.

Need-Based Scholarships

Available for students demonstrating financial need or from under-represented regions.

University-Specific Scholarships

Many Chinese universities offer their own funding schemes to attract international students.

Government-Funded Scholarships

The Chinese government and its agencies offer scholarships for international students to study in China

Private & Organisation Scholarships

Sponsored by companies, foundations or international organisations supporting study in China.
