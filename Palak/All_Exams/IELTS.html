Your Guide to IELTS Success

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is one of the world’s most trusted exams for study, work, and migration. Accepted by 11,000+ organizations worldwide, it evaluates your English skills in Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking.

Available in Academic and General Training formats, IELTS can be taken on paper, computer, or online. Scores are reported on a 0–9 band scale and remain valid for 2 years.

Four sections: Listening, Reading, Writing & Speaking

Total test duration: 2 hours 45 minutes

Scoring scale: 0–9 bands (valid for 2 years)

Test Format

Listening | Reading | Writing | Speaking

Scoring System

0–9 band scale (in 0.5 increments)

Duration

2 hours 45 minutes (total test time)

IELTS Exam Pattern & Scoring System

Understanding the IELTS format helps you prepare effectively and maximise your band score.

Listening

30 minutes + 10 min transfer time

40 questions across four recordings; understand conversations, monologues, opinions and detailed info.

Reading

60 minutes, 40 questions

3 passages; Academic texts for Academic version, and everyday reading for General Training.

Writing

60 minutes – 2 tasks

Task 1 & Task 2; task types differ for Academic vs General Training. Assessed on coherence, grammar, vocabulary, task response.

Speaking

11-14 minutes

Face-to-face interview (or video-call where applicable), part introduction, cue card, discussion.

9.0

Maximum Band Score

  • Each section scored 0-9 bands
  • Bands reported in 0.5 increments
  • Overall band is average of four section bands, rounded to nearest half-band.
Note: Listening, Reading, Writing are done on the same day (no breaks between them); Speaking may be scheduled up to a week before or after.
