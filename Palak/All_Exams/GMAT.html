Your Gateway to GMAT Focus Success

The GMAT Focus Edition is the latest version of the GMAT exam, tailored for efficiency and relevance. It evaluates your Quantitative Reasoning, Verbal Reasoning, and Data Insights skills to measure how you tackle real business challenges.

Scored between 205-805, this format is shorter and more targeted, making it ideal for aspiring business school candidates who want quality and precision.

Three core sections: Quant, Verbal & Data Insights

45 minutes per section with 21-23 questions each

No separate essay; Data Insights replaces Integrated Reasoning

Test Format

Quant | Verbal | Data Insights

Scoring System

205–805 total score (increments of 10)

Duration

2 hours 15 minutes + 10-min optional break

GMAT Focus Exam Pattern & Scoring System

Understanding the GMAT Focus Edition structure helps you prepare effectively and manage time strategically.

Quantitative Reasoning

21 Questions, 45 Minutes

Focuses on problem-solving, algebra, arithmetic, and applied math skills

Verbal Reasoning

23 Questions, 45 Minutes

Assesses critical reasoning, reading comprehension, and sentence correction

Data Insights

20 Questions, 45 Minutes

Evaluates data interpretation, analysis, and integration of information

805

Maximum Score

  • Total Score : 205–805 (increments of 10)
  • Section Scores : Scaled and contribute equally
  • Analytical Writing : Removed in Focus Edition
Note: GMAT Focus Edition is adaptive at the section level, and the Analytical Writing section has been removed for this version.
