USA
Processing Time 2-4 Weeks

The USA offers numerous loan options for students, including federal and private loans. International students typically need a U.S. co-signer. Understanding your financial health is key, as lenders assess your credit score, income, and repayment ability.

Requirement details

  • A credit score of at least 600 is required; higher scores improve approval chances and lower interest rates.
  • Proof of income is needed, such as pay stubs, tax returns, or recent bank statements, to show the ability to repay.
  • Verification of employment status or a job offer letter may be required, especially for recent graduates.
  • A debt-to-income (DTI) ratio below 43% is preferred, as it indicates better financial stability.
  • A cosigner may be necessary for international students, ideally someone living in the U.S.
  • Proof of university enrollment or admission is required.

Choose Your Loan Option

Compare loan options side by side to find the perfect fit for your financial situation

Co-Signer Required

Team Up for Better Terms

If you have a limited credit history, a co-signer with a strong credit profile can help you secure the loan. The co-signer will be responsible for repayment if you fail to pay.

No Co-signer Required

Go Solo with Independence

If you have a strong credit score or income, you may be able to apply for a loan without the need for a co-signer. This gives you the freedom to manage the loan on your own.

Collateral Required

Asset-Backed Security

Some loan providers may require collateral (such as property, savings, or other assets) to secure the loan. This can help reduce the risk for the lender and may lead to better loan terms.

Non - Collateral

No Assets Required

If you have a strong credit score or income, you may be able to apply for a loan without the need for a co-signer. This gives you the freedom to manage the loan on your own.
